5. Assessment

What's working

EMA_FPIP (PF 1.93, N=18): First pullback in play with structural targets to PDH/exp_high/session_high. This is the only strategy showing real edge under honest conditions. The expansion-high and PDH targets are natural levels for impulse continuation.

SC_SNIPER (PF 1.39, N=5): Breakout-retest with measured move targets. Promising but only 5 trades — needs more data.

The friction problem persists

Even with 100% structural targets, the combined system is deeply negative (PF 0.69). Median stop distances of 0.29–0.81% of entry mean 16bps round-trip friction consumes 20–55% of the risk budget.

The strategies that work (EMA_FPIP) have the tightest stops (0.43% median risk) but the highest R:R targets (median 3.0). The strategies that fail have similar stops but lower R:R targets.

The real question: is ORH_FBO_V2 broken or just bleeding from friction?

ORH_FBO_V2_A has 294 trades (54% of the portfolio) at PF 0.66. It dominates the results. With 0.44% median risk and ~0.28R of friction per trade, the strategy needs ~58% WR at 1:1 just to break even. It's hitting 39%.

This suggests the entry/stop/target logic itself may need auditing — not just the economics. A failed-breakout short to ORL should work in theory. Either the detection is too loose, or the stop placement is wrong, or the ORH level identification isn't precise enough.
