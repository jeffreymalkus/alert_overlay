5. Diagnosis

The truth about these strategies

With honest exits and real friction, every single strategy is underwater. The combined system loses -204.4R across 973 trades (PF 0.70).

The only bright spot is EMA_FPIP structural-target trades: 18 trades, PF 1.93, +7.4R — but it's a tiny sample, and the 65 non-structural trades lose -13.4R.

Three compounding problems

1. Friction kills tight stops. With 0.5–0.8% median risk and 16bps round-trip cost, friction consumes 20–35% of every R. This is the structural floor — no amount of signal quality fixes this without wider stops.

2. Structural targets are scarce. 44% of trades couldn't find a real structural level to exit at. Without those phantom targets, these trades bleed via stop and EOD exits.

3. Even structural targets underperform. The 542 structural-target trades have PF 0.68 — worse than the no-target group (PF 0.72). The targets that do exist may be too close (tight R:R) or at levels that don't hold.

What this means for the path forward

These strategies, as currently designed with their entry logic, stop placement, and target selection, do not have positive expectancy under realistic conditions. The previous PF of 1.31 was entirely a product of zero-friction simulation plus 44% of trades hitting fabricated targets.

To build something that works, the fundamental economics need to change: wider stops (so friction is <10% of R), and entries that produce directional edge strong enough to overcome realistic exit mechanics.
